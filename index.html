The objective of Bike Box is to repair bikes and help people without easily accessible transport like a bike or trike. The purpose of the website is to describe what the Bike Box is and to inform people on what their about. In 2013, we launched our popular "Celebrate Bronzeville" Rides, focusing on the themes of Art & Architecture, History, and Sustainability. The Bronzeville Bike Box is a 20-foot shipping container repurposed to fill a need for accessible and affordable bike sales and express repair services.

About

CELEBRATE BRONZEVILLE BY BIKE

Our "Celebrate Bronzeville" rides take place three times a month from June through October. Rides focus on the themes of Art & Architecture, History, and Sustainability. Our ride leaders are experts in their respective fields and passionate about showing off Bronzeville. All rides are completely free to the public and meet and end at the Bronzeville Bike Box (NW corner of 51st & Calumet).

Classes

To our sisters of all shapes and sizes, the Bronzeville Bike Box is proud to bring back our Sister Cycles Program, a *free* bike maintenance class for women. Classes take place at the Bronzeville Bike Box (51st & Calumet) the 1st and 3rd Saturday of each month from 9a-12p. Bring your bike and your self. If you don't have a bike, one will be provided for you to wrench on.
